Essential Travel Tips for Dubai

Understanding the Culture

Dubai is a modern city with a rich Islamic heritage. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, especially during Ramadan.

  • Dress Code: Dress modestly in public places. Women's clothing should cover shoulders and knees.
  • Public Behavior: Public displays of affection are generally frowned upon.
  • Alcohol Consumption: Alcohol is available in licensed hotels and restaurants.
  • Photography: Seek permission before photographing people.

Dubai's Weather

Dubai has a desert climate with hot summers and mild winters.

  • Summer (May to September): Extremely hot and humid, with temperatures often exceeding 40°C (104°F).
  • Winter (November to March): Pleasant weather with temperatures ranging from 15°C to 25°C (59°F to 77°F).

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Dubai is during the winter months (November to March) when the weather is most comfortable for outdoor activities.

Dos and Don'ts

Here are some quick guidelines to help you make the most of your trip:

Dos

  • Do try local cuisine.
  • Do bargain in the souks (traditional markets).
  • Do respect religious customs.

Don'ts

  • Don't litter.
  • Don't drink alcohol in public.
  • Don't forget sunscreen.
